Top Methods for Earning Honor and Conquest Points in WoW

In WoW, earning Honor and Conquest points is essential to gear up and climb the PvP ranks. Whether you're looking for quick rewards or aiming for higher-tier gear, these are the most effective ways to earn these valuable currencies in Patch 11.0.7, "The War Within."

Honor Points

  • Battlegrounds: Participate in random or rated battlegrounds to earn a steady stream of Honor points. Rated battlegrounds offer higher rewards!
  • World PvP: Activate War Mode and engage in world PvP encounters. Completing objectives in the open world rewards Honor points.
  • Weekly Quests: Complete weekly PvP quests for a good chunk of Honor points. These quests can be done in both battlegrounds and world PvP zones.

Conquest Points

  • Rated Arenas: Competing in rated arenas is one of the most efficient ways to earn Conquest points. Higher ratings provide better rewards!
  • Rated Battlegrounds: Similar to arenas, rated battlegrounds offer substantial Conquest point rewards based on your performance.
  • Weekly Quests: Conquest points are awarded for completing weekly PvP objectives, which can include a combination of rated battlegrounds, arenas, and other PvP content.
  • Event Participation: Some in-game events may offer Conquest points as rewards. Keep an eye on the latest events and participate for bonus Conquest.

Arena Comps and Strategies

In World of Warcraft, choosing the right arena composition and implementing effective strategies can make all the difference between victory and defeat. Whether you're jumping into 2v2 or 3v3 arenas, knowing the best team setups and tactics will help you climb the ranks.

In 2v2 & 3v3 arenas, the synergy between your teammate and yourself is crucial. In the arena, team compositions can vary depending on the desired strategy, Some of the most effective 2's and 3's compositions include:

  1. Assassination Rogue + Holy Paladin + Fire Mage - A powerful setup with excellent control, burst, and sustained pressure from all three players.
  2. Unholy Death Knight + Restoration Shaman + Elemental Shaman - Offers high damage, strong healing, and crowd control, perfect for both offensive and defensive strategies.
  3. Affliction Warlock + Restoration Druid + Beast Mastery Hunter - Provides sustained pressure and healing, with the Hunter offering utility and CC, and the Warlock dealing consistent damage.
  4. Devastation Evoker + Mistweaver Monk + Retribution Paladin - A solid composition that brings a lot of healing and high burst potential to quickly down enemies.
  5. Subtlety Rogue + Discipline Priest - A classic crowd control and healing combo, with high burst damage and great sustain.
  6. Fury Warrior + Holy Paladin - A durable tank and healer duo with strong offensive pressure and excellent healing output.
  7. Frost Mage + Restoration Druid - A great kite-and-control composition with high burst potential and strong healing over time.
  8. Windwalker Monk + Discipline Priest - The Monk provides high mobility and burst, while the Discipline Priest excels at healing and pressure through atonement.

Remember: The key to success in 2v2 and 3v3 is not just the composition, but how well you communicate and coordinate with your team. Practice makes perfect!

PvP Tips and Tricks

Want to improve your game? Learn how to track crowd control, use focus macros, and manage your cooldowns more effectively with our expert tips.

Global Cooldown (GCD)
The Global Cooldown is a shared cooldown applied to most abilities in WoW. After using an ability, you must wait for the GCD to reset before using another ability.
Crowd Control (CC)
Crowd Control refers to any ability that temporarily disables, stuns, or incapacitates an enemy player, preventing them from acting. Common CC types include stuns, silences, and snares.
Interrupt
Interrupt is an ability that stops an enemy from casting a spell. Interrupts are essential for shutting down key enemy abilities, especially in arenas or battlegrounds.
Healing Reduction
Healing reduction is a debuff that decreases the amount of healing a player receives. Common sources of healing reduction include abilities like Wounding Strike and Unholy Blight.
Peel
Peeling refers to the act of interrupting or mitigating the focus of an opponent on an ally, usually by applying crowd control, debuffs, or drawing aggro to protect a teammate.
Focus Target
Focus Target is a feature in World of Warcraft that allows players to set a specific target for abilities or macros without switching their primary target. This is especially useful for crowd control or healing a specific ally.

Understanding these core terms is crucial for improving your PvP performance. The ability to manage your GCD, interrupt key spells, use CC effectively, and peel for your teammates can turn the tide in many combat situations.

  • Interrupting Key Spells: Knowing when and how to interrupt a crucial enemy spell can prevent devastating damage or healing. For instance, interrupting a healer's Greater Heal can secure a kill for your team.
  • Effective CC Usage: Timing your crowd control abilities is vital for shutting down enemy healers or damage dealers. Practice coordinated CC chains to maximize pressure on your opponents.
  • Peeling for Your Team: Whether it's using a stun or slowing down an enemy, always be ready to protect your team. Effective peeling keeps your teammates alive and helps control the battlefield.
