Most Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration and one of the most well-known Founding Fathers. He later became the third president of the United States.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence and a key figure in the Continental Congress. He went on to become the second president of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A well-known diplomat, inventor, and writer. Franklin was a respected figure both in America and internationally.
John Hancock
The president of the Continental Congress at the time of the signing, Hancock is famous for his bold and large signature on the document.
Samuel Adams
A political leader and one of the driving forces behind the American Revolution, known for his role in organizing the Boston Tea Party.
Richard Henry Lee
A member of the Continental Congress who made the motion for independence, which led to the drafting of the Declaration.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four of the major American founding documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
